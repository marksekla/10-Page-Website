Alexandria

Alexandria, the second-largest city in Egypt and a well-liked tourist attraction, is situated on the Mediterranean Sea. You may go to Alexandria from Cairo in a single day or stay overnight. The Serapeum, Alexandria National Museum, Corniche, Stanley Bridge, Catacombs of Kom El Shoqafa, and Montazah Palace Gardens are a some of the city's top attractions. Egypt's Mediterranean port city is called Alexandria. It was home to a famous library and a lighthouse that was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World during the Hellenistic era. Bibliotheca Alexandrina, a futuristic disc-shaped building, is the library's current incarnation. Sand beaches, vintage cafes, and Greco-Roman sites are all found in the city. Its seaside Qaitbay Citadel from the fifteenth century is now a museum.
