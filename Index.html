Egypt

Egypt, which connects northeastern Africa and the Middle East, has a long history. Along the rich Nile River Valley are several sites that date back thousands of years, such as the enormous Pyramids of Giza, the Great Sphinx, the hieroglyph-lined Karnak Temple, and the Valley of the Kings tombs at Luxor. Ottoman sites like Muhammad Ali Mosque and the Egyptian Museum, which is filled with artefacts, can be found in Cairo, the country's capital. Egypt is often divided into two sections: Towards the south is Upper Egypt, while in the north is Lower Egypt. The Nile runs from south to north, thus the names of the parts.
